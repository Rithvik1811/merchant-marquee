**Phase 2 research note (RR, see `docs/TECHNICAL_DOCUMENTATION.md` Â§5.6/Â§5.7 for full detail):** four Opus research passes (cinematography vocabulary, AI-video failure modes, grounded-generation technique, budget-allocation heuristics) landed three design revisions to the original single-call Shot-List Agent spec, now written into Â§5.6/Â§5.7 as the spec of record:

## Backstory-First Script Restructuring + Anti-Rhyme Fix (branch `video-gen-fidelity`, RR)
*Not a numbered PHASE_PLAN.md phase â€” a targeted script-quality fix spanning Concept Agent / Hook-Checker / Treatment Agent / Shot-List Agent, opened after a real live pipeline run kept producing flaw-led, pain-first, sing-song scripts despite two earlier attempts at a narrower "positive framing" prompt tweak.*

**Trigger:** a real full-pipeline run (`derisk/outputs/full_pipeline_live_story_arc.log`, 2026-07-11) produced this winning script for a leather backpack ad: *"Other bags hide this 2cm scuff, not ours. You're terrified of scratching a pristine bag, right? She slings it over her shoulder on her way out the door, and that little mark just blends in. See this strap? It threads through a solid rivet, not a cheap snap. Stop babying your gear. Grab yours."* Leads with the product's own flaw, frames the viewer's own insecurity as the hook, and has sing-song word-final rhyme risk (e.g. "gear"/"here"-class endings). Confirmed as a real, reproduced, unaddressed complaint, not a one-off.

**Root cause (fable-model web-grounded research, not guessed) â€” three mechanisms, not one:**
1. `agents/concept_agent.py`'s HOOK STRENGTH prompt block never forbade flaw-led/pain-first hooks or rhyme.
2. `agents/concept_agent.py` has a **deterministic validator**, `_hook_has_number_or_contrast()`, that mechanically rejects any hook line lacking a digit or a contrast marker â€” so even a warm, human-centric opening gets bounced back into the re-prompt loop and rewritten toward pain/contrast, independent of the prompt wording.
3. `agents/hook_checker.py`'s scoring rubric's own explicit 5/5 (top-score) exemplar is a pain-tied-to-a-number claim ("Your coffee is cold in 12 minutes. Mine isn't.") â€” so even if the writer stopped producing flaw-led hooks, the Meta-Critic would keep scoring pain/number-led hooks highest and discarding human-centric opens as "weaker," by rubric construction.

**Research-backed structural target (see session record for full citations â€” StoryBrand SB7, narrative-transportation literature, Binet & Field's *Long and Short of It*, and specific award-winning spots: Apple "Misunderstood," Google "Parisian Love," Dove "Real Beauty Sketches," Extra Gum "Sarah & Juan"):** open on an in-medias-res human moment with the product visibly in-scene but not yet pitched (beat 1) â†’ product enters by name with its benefit framed as the payoff of that moment (beats 2-3) â†’ imperfection-category truths, if used at all, appear only *after* a positive beat has landed, framed as earned character not defect (directly backed by the "blemishing effect" research â€” negative info only aids persuasion when it *follows* positive info, never precedes it) â†’ single CTA close. Short-form nuance (TikTok's own creative-best-practice data): this must be a *compressed* human moment (~3s), not a brandless establishing film â€” the product must stay visible in the first 3 seconds.

**Rhyme fix:** LLM self-assessment of its own rhymes is unreliable (research cites ~54% accuracy); use a deterministic phonetic check instead, in the same re-prompt-loop pattern `_lifted_run`/`_stacked_compound_match` already establish in this file.

**Decisions confirmed with project owner (2026-07-11):**
- **Hero-shot duration scales to target ad length**, not a flat 10-15s floor â€” the research flagged that `HERO_SHOT_MIN_DURATION_SEC=10.0` plus a new ~3s backstory opening plus a ~2.5-4s CTA already exceeds a 15s ad before any other shot exists. For 15s-target ads, scale the hero window down (e.g. `min(HERO_SHOT_MAX_DURATION_SEC, target*0.5)`-style); the full 10-15s range is reserved for 30s-target ads.
- **Rhyme detection uses the `pronouncing` package** (new dependency â€” MIT-licensed, ~5KB, wraps the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary, gives linguistically-correct rhyme-unit matching via `rhyming_part()`) rather than a dependency-free heuristic-only approach.
